Welcome To $enturion

An Andriod financial management app designed to help users keep track of everyday finances in a convenient way.




Vision Statement



What the project is?

Our goal is to help our users be more aware of their spending practices and identify areas in which they may be able to make changes. To achieve this goal, we want users to be able to add spending records and be as specific about these expenditures as possible. By incorporating categories, we can help users identify and consolidate certain types of purchases. Whenever a user desires we can display up to date financial records, as well as allow the users to view specific types of transactions based on those specific categories, amounts, retailer name, and other criteria.

Who the users are?

Our target audience for this app consists of members of the working populace, as well as teenagers in the workforce. Also, these people must have access to an android mobile device in order to be able to use our app. However, persons who have a vested interest in managing their finances and planning for the future may be more interested in our app. Specific features built into the app may also be particularly useful for certain demographics.

Why the project is valuable?

We believe this app will be useful because many people are unaware of where their money goes over the course of a month or more. Additionally, people may be interested in tracking finances for various reasons. Some users may be interested in creating a complete budget, while others may only need to record specific types of expenses. By allowing users to track their spending when itâ€™s most convenient to them we hope to create an app that is an effective solution to any of these problems.

How we call it success?

Our app will be considered successful based on three criteria. First being, we want our users to prefer using our app as opposed to any other form of financial tracking methods. We will determine this by recording consecutive uses and we will review this data periodically. Secondly, we want our app to be easy to use. We should get over 50% positive feedback through the questionnaire survey. If our application is too difficult to use it will no longer be convenient and therefore unsuccessful. Third, our users need to be able to keep track of their finance by group. We can test this by asking users are they aware of which aspects they mostly spent on.


Features



Income and Expense Record

You can create income or expense records with amount, date, description and catogery. Successfully creating a record will redirect you to the corresponding page where you can view your records in the time order and the total amount resides on the top of the screen. You are also able to delete the records.


Catogery Integration

There are a list of categories you can choose from when creating new records or you can add new customized category. The record with a category will be input on the main page under that category. You can easily group your incomes and expenses using the categories and keep track of your money for each type of transaction since all the categories come with the total amount.


Budget All In One

On opening the app, you can see the default budget holding your list of categories, where clicking one category gives you the collection of incomes and expenses belonging to it. You can edit the budget name, initial amount and starting date. The amount left for this budget resides on the left of the initial amount. You can have a clear mind of how much you left to spend, then you won't go over your budget.


Dynamic Amount

On income and expense page, the total amount on the top is updated when you create or delete the records. These operations will also update the budget left amount on the main page, and the total amount associated to each category. The positive number stands for how much you earned and the negative number is how much you've spent.

Restrospective



Development skills learned

We learned how to use git more effectively, how to use layered architecture and how to build applications that use access seams instead of instantiating new objects all the time. We built the real database and learned how to communicate with it. We wrote a lot of tests including unit tests, integration tests, and system tests, by which we understood the importance of Test Driven Development. This experience definitely makes it easier for us to adapt to the industry where we need to implement real solutions in production.

Change from initial vision

We originally planned a much more in depth project when we had 5 group members. Our feature list contained lots of features that were "nice to haves" as we assumed that we would have time to build them. Once we learned that 2 of the members were no longer taking part we narrowed our project scope drastically to compensate. Moving from a full fledged budgeting application to more of a transaction recorder. The app was designed such that it could be extended in the future to contain all the original features we planned.

What to keep doing

We learned that completing work earlier is better for everyone. Development almost always came with some kind of problem to be overcome and if the core structure is completed well before the release date other team members can help solve those challenges, before they become a massive challenge. I also learned that having in person team meetings is beneficial when the decisions were made in less time and more members understood the goal. The communication among the team vastly increased the work efficiency.
